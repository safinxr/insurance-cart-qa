Estimating car insurance costs

Are you paying too much for car insurance? It's a question most drivers ask themselves. Knowing how to properly estimate how much you should be paying can help you when you're on the hunt for a new car insurance policy. Along with The GO Insurance's car insurance calculator and tool above, we've also compiled the following guide and other resources to help in your search for fair car insurance rates, whether it's for your personal vehicle or commercial vehicle insurance.

Key Takeaways

  • Your rates can vary by location, driving history and the car you drive.
  • In 2024, the estimated monthly rate for car insurance was about $147 or $880 for a six-month policy.
  • Every insurer has its own underwriting process, meaning quotes will vary widely from one company to another.
  • Compare quotes from multiple companies to get the best rates.

Information needed to get insurance quotes

In order to receive a car insurance quote, you'll need to have the same basic information handy. To speed up the process, make sure have the following ready to share

Driver's license

Name, address (or your vehicles garaging location), and birthday are required in addition to gender and marital status. Some insurance companies will also require your Driver License number.

Vehicle type

You need to know the year, make, model, and trim of your primary vehicle. While you don't need to know your vehicle identification number (VIN) during your quoting stage, you will eventually need it to buy your policy.

Other vehicle details

Vehicle status (owned or leased), primary use and average miles driven are typically asked for as well.

Insurance history

Have you had continuous coverage or are you currently uninsured? Some car insurance companies require at least six months of continuous auto insurance to issue a policy.

Additional information

Lastly, you'll be asked about any accidents or citations (excluding parking tickets), so having a clean driving record is ideal. Credit score and highest-level of education will also be needed to understand what kind of risk you might be.

Comprehensive and collision coverage overview

Both of these coverages protect your vehicle from physical damage. Your collision coverage protects your vehicle if you collide with another car or fixed object, whereas your comprehensive coverage protects your vehicle from weather-related incidents, animals, or vandalism. When paired together, these coverages are often collectively known as full coverage.

These coverages are not required by state law but can be required if you’re leasing or financing a vehicle. Both are also subject to a deductible — what you pay prior to your insurance company reimbursing you for any damage. How car insurance premiums are calculated

Every car insurance company looks at a driver's complete profile to set the policy's rates. A driver's profile includes several factors, some of which are in their control: maintaining a good driving record or driving an affordable car to insure. The amount a driver pays for insurance is based on several factors, including location, age, gender, marital status, credit score, vehicle, and auto insurance and driving history. The less risk posed by a client to an insurance provider, the lower their premium will be.
